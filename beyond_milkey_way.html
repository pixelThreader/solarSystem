GALAXIES

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, planetary systems, and dust bound together by gravity. Beyond the Milky Way, there are billions of other galaxies, each with their own unique properties and mysteries.

NEBULAS

A nebula is a vast cloud of dust, hydrogen, and other gases in space, showcasing diverse shapes and colors. Emission nebulae glow brightly from nearby stars, reflection nebulae softly reflect starlight, while dark nebulae obscure distant stars. Planetary nebulae and supernova remnants reveal the life cycles of stars, from birth to explosive death.

BLACK HOLE

Black holes are cosmic entities with gravitational pull so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape them. Formed from the collapse of massive stars or through mergers, they exist in various sizes, including stellar-mass and supermassive varieties at the hearts of galaxies. Their immense gravity warps spacetime profoundly.

Blackhole

DARK MATTER

Dark matter is a mysterious substance that makes up about 27% of the universe's mass-energy content. It doesn't give off any light and is super sneaky, only interacting with regular matter through gravity. Even though we can't see it directly, we know it's there because it affects how galaxies move and stick together. Scientists are still trying to figure out what exactly it's made of.
